Mission

Accuo strives to provide an intuitive, affordable, and accurate means of performing lumbar puncture by bringing advanced imaging to the physician’s fingertips. Our mission is to ameliorate the uncertainty and risks associated with what should be a painless and straightforward diagnostic procedure.

Problem

Lumbar punctures (LPs) are a clinical diagnostic technique involving the collection of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) from the subarachnoid space in a patient. In order to successfully collect CSF, clinicians have to blindly yet accurately navigate a needle through the L3-L5 intervertebral space, epidural space, dura mater, and arachnoid mater whilst avoiding nerves, blood vessels, and bone. More than 400,000 LP’s are performed annually but nearly 23.3% end in failure due to the myriad of challenges. These failures lead to misdiagnoses, treatment delays, and subsequent unnecessary and dangerous follow-up procedures. Patients with excess adipose tissue between skin and target structure, namely scoliotic and obese patients, suffer a significantly increased probability of LP failure.

Our Solution

Accuo is developing a novel ultrasound imaging technology to ameliorate the current issues with LPS and other deep needle placements. Our device delivers the world’s first depth-independent, high-resolution imaging via an ultrasound sensor positioned at the tip of a needle. A simple sweeping of the needle produces an image of the surrounding tissue, up to 6 cm ahead of the tip. The Accuo “smart needle” maintains image quality that current topical ultrasound probes lose at certain depths. With this device, clinicians can identify obstacles before hitting them and reduce the number of failed attempts. Accuo aims to save patients from complications, unnecessary tests, and delays in treatment or diagnosis. Ultimately, the entire healthcare system will benefit from the more efficient and reliable lumbar puncture procedures. While Accuo is intended to target lumbar punctures, the technology that enables Accuo to work is applicable across a wide range of medical procedures, from epidurals to central line placements. Accuo’s novel idea consists of using an ultrasound element embedded at the tip of a needle that tracks the movement of the needle. This movement is then converted into an ultrasound image through Accuo’s synthetic aperture algorithm, which allows physicians to see both a B-mode image and real time distance to bone information as they advance the needle.
